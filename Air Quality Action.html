About Air Quality

Air quality affects our health every day. Pollutants like PM2.5, PM10, NO₂, SO₂, CO, and O₃ can cause respiratory illnesses, heart disease, and other health issues. Monitoring the Air Quality Index (AQI) helps individuals make informed decisions about outdoor activities and protective measures.

AQI Categories

AQI Category Health Advice
0 - 50 Good Enjoy outdoor activities.
51 -100 Moderate Sensitive individuals should monitor symptoms.
101-150 Unhealthy for Sensitive Groups Limit prolonged outdoor activity.
151-200 Unhealthy Wear a mask and reduce outdoor exposure.
201-300 Very Unhealthy Avoid outdoor exercise.
301+ Hazardous Stay indoors whenever possible.

Major Air Pollutants

PM2.5

Fine particles that penetrate deep into the lungs.

PM10

Dust and pollen particles affecting breathing.

NO₂

Mainly emitted from vehicle exhaust.

O₃

Ground-level ozone can irritate the respiratory system.
